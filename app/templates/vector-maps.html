-F.A.Q-

Frequently asked questions ?

We have provided a list of commonly asked questions to assist you..

Climate change is the global variation of the Earth's climate due mainly to human action, especially the emission of greenhouse gases.

The main greenhouse gases are carbon dioxide (CO2), methane (CH4), nitrous oxide (N2O) and fluorinated gases. But so far only carbon dioxide and methane results are shown in the resources. We expect to incorporate more data soon.

Climate change causes sea level rise, ocean acidification and coral bleaching, among other effects.

The main sources are the burning of fossil fuels for energy and transportation, deforestation, intensive agriculture and industry.

The carbon footprint is the total greenhouse gases emitted directly or indirectly by an individual, organization, event or product.

Climate change alters habitats, modifies migratory patterns and can lead to the extinction of species that are unable to adapt to rapid change.

Deforestation is the loss of forests and jungles. It contributes to climate change by reducing the Earth's ability to absorb CO2 and by releasing the carbon stored in trees.

  1. Energy efficiency at home: Use LED bulbs, unplug devices, and improve insulation.
  2. Transportation: Walk, bike, use public transport, or drive electric vehicles. Practice fuel-efficient driving.
  3. Diet: Reduce meat and dairy consumption, buy local and seasonal food, and minimize food waste.
  4. Reduce, reuse, recycle: Consume less, reuse items, and recycle properly.
  5. Renewable energy: Choose renewable energy or install solar panels if possible.
  6. Water conservation: Save water and use water-efficient fixtures.
  7. Awareness and support: Advocate for environmental policies, educate others, and engage in local sustainability efforts.
